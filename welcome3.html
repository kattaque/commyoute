About Our Project

We want to create a platform for transportation advocacy by collecting commuting data. Reliable access to transit has been found to be a major factor in determining upward mobility for low-income households : long, difficult commutes and inability to get to workplaces severely limits opportunities.

Safety Rankings and Criteria

Why we ranked it as such

These four factors were chosen based on Chapters 3 and 4 of the Transit Capacity and Quality of Service Manual (Third Edition, 2013), which focus on the concepts and methods affecting the quality of public transit service. There are other factors that the manual discusses such as accuracy of real-time bus arrival information, comfort and crowding inside the bus, and street traffic patterns. However, we decided to focus on four factors that would be easily quantifiable.

Criteria Description
Accessibility If the walking distance to the bus stop is over 0.25 miles, subtract 1 point.
Frequency If the time until the next bus is greater than 15 minutes, subtract 1 point.
Travel Time For longer commutes, if the bus time is greater than the car time by 1.5 magnitudes, subtract 1 point.
Convenience If there's more than one transfer to another bus, subtract 1 point.
